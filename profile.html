A workplace learning and performance practitioner specializing in various types of interpersonal communication; currently working for a national corporation as a communication instructor in public speaking, intercultural relations, and communication skills.

Existing Competencies

I am quite confortable in any traditional learning environment; as a student, an instructor, or a workplace practitioner. I like to think that in academia is where I belong. I owe one of my deepest core values to my alma mater, Northeastern Illinois University, because I am truly a life long learner. Professionally, I have accumulated over six years of work in higher education in roles such as: student worker, administrative assistant and, now, instructor. I have worked for the University of Wisconsin in Madison, the Illinios Institute of Technology in Chicago, and now, a national career college.

Specialty #1: Public Speaking

I currently train adults in the art of public speaking and I absolutely love it. My natural gift for gab has turned into a career of teaching others how to communicate effectively in most environments. Especially, when voice projection, inflection, eye contact, hand gestures and language choice are of the utmost concern. From my experience, I've learned that confidence in oneself is key. Also, applying existing knowledge to the task of developing a speech and relating it to a target audience is also very important for those who are seeking to increase their presentation skills. I find that my style of teaching public speaking is to coach students through a process of building confidence, assessing their existing knowledge that is relevant to their speech topics, identifying strong connections with their audiences, and using appropriate language that increases their credibility.

Specialty #2: Intercultural Communication

Intercultural communication is another topic in communication that I specialize in. I have been fortunate enough to learn it, live it, and pass it on. The aim of successful intercultural relations is to allow the experience of learning different cultures to transform oneself. Inevitably, intercultural interaction will affect a person's outlook on life, provided they actively reflect on their experiences. Gaining an appreciation for positive intercultural relations is a key component to achieving a healthy global perspective. It fosters confidence in communication through empathy, which creates a human connection that cannot be easily broken. Of course, most people, naturally, favor their own ethnic group. However, ethnocentrism (a feeling of ethnic superiority) should never be harnessed or encouraged.

Specialty #3: Communication Skills

Knowing how to communicate, naturally, is a given. However, I advocate that communicating effectively is a skill that needs to be acquired. The successful sending and receiving of messages depends on many factors and the desired result is efficiency. I emphasize to my students that context coupled with receiver knowledge and expectations provide a healthy foundation for a successful communicative exchange. Skill building involves a conscious effort to: arm oneself with basic knowledge of how to communicate effectively in various environments, practice in the real world, and to develop one's own style. Also, I believe that forgiving oneself for insignificant flaws in everyday speech, balanced with a valuable effort to reduce occurrences of miscommunication due to these flaws, is extremely important.
